Let's denote the numbers on the sides of the cube as a,b,c,d,e,f, where a and b are on opposite sides, c and d are on opposite sides, and e and f are on opposite sides. The condition given is that the products of numbers on each pair of opposite sides are all the same.

Let this common product be P. Then we have the following equations:

ab = P

cd = P

ef = P

To find the lowest possible sum S = a + b + c + d + e + f, we need to consider integer pairs (a,b), (c,d), (e,f) such that ab = cd = ef = P and a, b, c, d, e, f are positive integers.

Let's explore the potential pairs to minimize S:

  1. (a,b) = (1,P), (c,d) = (2,P/2), (e,f) = (4,P/4):
    a + b + c + d + e + f = 1 + P + 2 + P/2 + 4 + P/4
    Simplifying this:
    S = 7 + P + P/2 + P/4
    S = 7 + (1 + 1/2 + 1/4)P
    S = 7 + 7/4 P
  2. To minimize S, choose P = 4 (the smallest integer that can be a product):
    S = 7 + 7/4 . 4 = 7+ 7 = 14
Verify:
  • ab = 1 . 4 = 4
  • cd = 2 . 2 = 4
  • ef = 4 . 1 = 4

Thus, P = 4 satisfies the condition. Therefore, the lowest possible sum of all the numbers on the sides of the cube is 14.
