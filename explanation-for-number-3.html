Let's analyze each statement to determine which is true:

Statement (a)
"The graph of a horizontal line can’t have any x-intercepts."
A horizontal line has the equation y = c for some constant c. It can have x-intercepts if c = 0 (i.e., the x-axis itself). Thus, this statement is false because a horizontal line can have an x-intercept when it lies on the x-axis.

Statement (b)
"The graph of a horizontal line can’t have a unique x-intercept, but may have more than one x-intercept."
As analyzed above, a horizontal line can either have no x-intercept (if c ≠ 0) or an infinite number of x-intercepts (if c = 0). Therefore, this statement is false because a horizontal line can have exactly one x-intercept if it lies on the x-axis and c = 0.

Statement (c)
"The graph of a parabola can’t have a unique x-intercept, but may have more than one x-intercept."
A parabola described by y = ax^2 + bx + c can have:
• No x-intercept if the discriminant b^2 − 4ac is negative.
• Exactly one x-intercept if the discriminant b^2 − 4ac is zero (a double root).
• Two distinct x-intercepts if the discriminant b^2 − 4ac is positive.
Thus, this statement is false because a parabola can have a unique x-intercept.

Statement (d)

"The graph of a polynomial of degree three or higher must have at least one x-intercept."
A polynomial of degree three or higher is a continuous function and, according to the Fundamental Theorem of Algebra, it must have at least one real root (x-intercept). This is due to the fact that polynomials of odd degree (like degree three) have at least one real root since they extend to ±∞ as x → ±∞. For even degree polynomials higher than two, they can also have real roots.

Statement (e)

"Either none are true or more than one are true."
From our analysis, we see that:

  • Statements (a), (b), and (c) are false.
  • Statement (d) is true.
Therefore, statement (d) is the only true statement.
